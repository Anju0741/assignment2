Welcome to Kerala

God's Own Country - Kerala

Kerala, located in the southwestern part of India, is a tropical paradise renowned for its pristine backwaters, lush green landscapes, serene beaches, and vibrant cultural heritage. The state is a treasure trove of natural beauty and traditions that date back centuries.

Known for its Ayurveda, exotic wildlife, and picturesque hill stations, Kerala is a haven for both nature lovers and adventurers. From the spice-scented plantations of Thekkady to the serene beaches of Varkala, Kerala offers a unique blend of relaxation and exploration.

Top Destinations in Kerala

Alleppey (Alappuzha)

Known as the "Venice of the East," Alleppey is famous for its tranquil backwaters, houseboats, and lush greenery.

Munnar

Nestled in the Western Ghats, Munnar is a beautiful hill station known for its rolling tea plantations and misty mountains.

Kochi (Cochin)

A bustling port city, Kochi is a blend of historical sites like Fort Kochi, vibrant markets, and cultural diversity.

Thekkady

Home to the Periyar Wildlife Sanctuary, Thekkady offers rich biodiversity and adventurous jungle safaris.

Varkala

A serene beach destination, Varkala is famous for its dramatic cliffs, yoga retreats, and stunning sunsets.

Wayanad

With its dense forests, waterfalls, and spice plantations, Wayanad is a nature lover's paradise.

Festivals of Kerala

Kerala's cultural calendar is filled with vibrant festivals that celebrate its heritage, religion, and traditions. Some of the most famous festivals include:

  • Onam: The harvest festival celebrated with flower carpets, grand feasts, and traditional dances.
  • Thrissur Pooram: A spectacular temple festival with grand elephant processions and fireworks.
  • Vishu: Celebrated as Kerala's New Year with prayers, feasts, and traditional rituals.
